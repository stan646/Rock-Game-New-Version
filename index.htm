Guidelines for Playing Rock, Scissors, Paper

Rock, Scissors, Paper is a simple and fun hand game often used to make decisions or just for entertainment. Follow these guidelines to understand how to play: Objective of the Game The goal is to choose a hand gesture (Rock, Scissors, or Paper) that defeats your opponent's gesture according to the rules.

How to Play

1. Players: The game is played between You and Computer.

2. Preparation: - Each player makes a fist with one hand. - The game begins with both players click, "Rock, scissors, paper, shoot!" or a similar countdown.

3. Gestures: On "shoot," each player simultaneously shows one of three gestures with their hand: - Rock: A closed fist. - Scissors: Two fingers extended, mimicking the shape of scissors. - Paper: An open hand with fingers extended

4. Winning Rules: The outcome is determined as follows: - Rock crushes Scissors: Rock wins. - Scissors cut Paper: Scissors win. - Paper covers Rock: Paper wins. - Same Gesture: It's a tie; play again.

Example Scenarios - Player 1 shows Rock, Player 2 shows Scissors -> Player 1 wins (Rock crushes Scissors). - Player 1 shows Scissors, Player 2 shows Paper -> Player 1 wins (Scissors cut Paper). - Player 1 shows Paper, Player 2 shows Paper -> Tie, replay the round.

Tips for Fun Play

- Be quick with your gesture to keep the game exciting. - Add a best-of-three or best-of-five format to determine the overall winner. - Use the game to decide small disagreements or just for a quick, friendly competition! Now you're ready to play!
